# cryoDRGN J1497 latent landscape (5-class)

PCA/UMAP visualization of the 10-D latent space from the J1497 z10 run
(`results_cryodrgn/J1497_real/train/z.100.pkl`, 230,396 particles).
Generated by `scripts/cryodrgn/cryodrgn_landscape.py`.

Key finding: The latent density shows 2-3 peaks, not 5. P9 and P10 do not
appear as distinct density regions in any panel.

Note: UMAP was not generated for J1497 (redundant given identical image set and
nearly identical latent to J1442; PC1 r = 0.898 between the two runs).

## Key metrics

- PC1 explains 15.5% of standardized latent variance; PC2 explains 11.2%.
  (identical to J1442 — same image set, same dominant conformational axis)
- K=5 GMM: min component separation 0.88 SD, mean 1.95 SD.
  (well below 2 SD; 5 components heavily overlap in the latent density)
- PC1 marginal shows 2-3 peaks, not 5: P9 and P10 are not visible as distinct modes.
- K=10 fine-grain: all 10 ellipses tile the same 3 density peaks as J1442;
  no 4th or 5th distinct structural state is present.
- Panel B (CryoSPARC coloring): P9 particles cluster at P8's position; P10 at P6's.

## GMM peak z-vectors (`z_gmm_peaks.txt`)

Five rows, each 10 values: the 5 GMM component means in the 10-D latent space.
Note that two pairs of means are very close together (P9~P8, P10~P6).

**Decode with `cryodrgn eval_vol` (requires GPU):**
```bash
cryodrgn eval_vol results_cryodrgn/J1497_real/train/weights.pkl \
  --config results_cryodrgn/J1497_real/train/config.yaml \
  --zfile results_cryodrgn/J1497_real/landscape/z_gmm_peaks.txt \
  --Apix 0.83 \
  -o results_cryodrgn/J1497_real/landscape/peak_volumes
```
Expected: vol_P9 ~ vol_P8; vol_P10 ~ vol_P6.
